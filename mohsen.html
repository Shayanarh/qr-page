MOHSEN ABBASI, 21 YEARS OLD — ISFAHAN
Killed on January 8, 2026 in Najafabad during nationwide protests
“NEW YORK IS REALLY VERY BEAUTIFUL”

Mohsen Abbasi was a 21-year-old young man from Najafabad in Isfahan Province. He was killed on January 8, 2026 during nationwide protests in Iran after being directly shot by the Islamic Republic’s repressive forces.

Mohsen's heart was full of love, tenderness, and the quiet poetry of a life he deserved to live. He wrote about the person he loved with words that now make his loss even more heartbreaking.

“Her beauty made you freeze in place, as if for a few seconds you were involuntarily drawn into simply watching her. Beside her, even I seemed beautiful.”

“People become truly beautiful only beside one person; then it can be said that the world, whatever it is, is beautiful.”

“In her eyes were thousands of coffee trees that interpreted my sleeplessness.”

These are not the words of someone who should have been buried so young. They are the words of someone who should have lived, loved, married, and built a beautiful life.

But the regime did not allow him that future. Instead it stole his life and turned those dreams into grief.

In Iran hope and fear live together. As he wrote: “We have not fallen behind in life; we are just building from zero the things that many people had from the moment they were born.”

“Our instrument seems stuck out of tune,” he said, describing the feeling of living in a place where dreams are constantly interrupted.

Yet even then he still carried hope: “Honestly, I am a child whose dreams are bigger than his size.”

Now he is dead, killed by the Islamic Republic regime, and all of his hopes are buried underground.

If we do not mourn the war in our country in the way others expect, it is because this government has already taken the lives of tens of thousands of young people.

We need international help so that we can stay alive and safe.

Persian Academic and Cultural Society
