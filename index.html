Arefeh Beygi, 20 years old, Isfahan
She was killed in the Isfahan protests on Thursday, January 8, 2026
“I hope I don’t leave this world before I’ve emigrated, seen the Northern Lights, gone to New York, seen Venice, been to Paris, and visited Switzerland.”

She shared many wishes on Instagram, including: “I really hope this year will truly be a good one for me, after everything I went through in 2026.” But before she could see any of those wishes come true, she was killed by the Islamic Republic regime that same year.

She once wrote, “Life is full of pain and suffering.” It is heartbreaking that a young girl had to feel this so deeply. In another country, a girl her age might be thinking about ordinary things, going to the gym, dancing, meeting friends, sitting in a café, and dreaming freely about her future. But she was forced to grow up in a place where even the most basic human rights were denied.

That is why her words cut so deeply: “We have never experienced a normal life with basic human rights.” It is painful to think that while so many young people in the world are allowed to live with freedom and dignity, she had to live under fear, oppression, and injustice simply because she was born in Iran under the Islamic Republic.

And yet, despite all that pain, she still had dreams. She hoped she would not leave this world before emigrating, before seeing the Northern Lights, before visiting New York, Venice, Paris, and Switzerland. These were not extravagant dreams. They were simple, human dreams, the kind every young person deserves to have.

But now, instead of living those dreams, she is under the ground, killed simply because she came out to ask for the most basic human rights. So when people speak only of war in our country, they should also remember the young women, young men, and children who were killed by our own terrorist government. Their stories matter. Their names matter. And the people of Iran need the world not to look away.

Persian Academic and Cultural Society
